FAQ

White Chiavari Chair Rental — Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a Chiavari chair and why is it the wedding standard?

A Chiavari chair (originally from Chiavari, Italy) is a lightweight ballroom chair with an open lattice back and four tapered legs. The design has been used at formal events since the early 1800s — its proportions photograph beautifully and disappear visually against tablecloths, which is why wedding photographers and planners default to it. Our white resin version is weather-resistant, stackable 10 high, and rated to 500 lb, so it holds up at both outdoor ceremonies and indoor receptions.

Do the chairs come with cushions?

Yes — every white Chiavari chair in our inventory comes with a matching ivory padded seat cushion. The cushions are inspected and wiped down before every rental; any cushion with visible wear is retired. If you prefer the uncushioned look (common for cocktail-hour chair stacks or ceremony-aisle chairs), we can accommodate that — just note it when booking.

How many chairs can I order and what's the minimum?

There's no strict minimum — we stage orders from around a dozen chairs to several hundred. Our inventory is large enough to handle events of 300+ guests with Chiavari chairs throughout. If you need an unusual quantity (under 20 or over 400), call us first so we can confirm availability on your date.

Does your crew set the chairs up in rows or around tables?

Both — and any mix. Share your floor plan (even a rough sketch) and we'll place chairs to your ceremony rows, round-table seating, banquet-table seating, or any hybrid. If your layout involves a ceremony-to-reception flip (chairs moved from rows to tables mid-event), discuss that with us at booking so we can plan crew time accordingly.

Can I mix Chiavari chairs with your other chair styles in one order?

Yes, and there's no mixing fee. A common approach is white Chiavari chairs at the head and sweetheart tables with Fanback or Resin Garden chairs filling the remaining seats — it creates a focal point without a large cost premium. We deliver all chair types on the same truck.

Are Chiavari chairs suitable for outdoor events?

Our white Chiavari chairs are resin (not wood), so they handle BC outdoor conditions well — no warping from humidity, no staining from light rain. For outdoor use on grass, we recommend pairing with our marquee tent to protect both the chairs and your guests if weather shifts. The chairs should not be left in standing water or direct UV for extended multi-day periods.

How far in advance should I book?

For summer Saturday weddings (May–September), 3–6 months ahead is recommended — Chiavari chairs are our most-requested item and peak weekends commit early. For off-season dates or weekday events, 4–6 weeks is usually fine. You can check live availability and hold your date with a 25% deposit via our online booking system at any time.
