## Patterns observed (so far)

1. **Weak on-page schema across competitors.** None of the top 4 appear to use `FAQPage` or `AggregateRating` schema. Forever adding these will provide immediate rich-snippet differentiation.
2. **Thin dedicated city pages.** No competitor has a dedicated `/surrey-party-rentals` vs. `/langley-party-rentals` URL pattern the way Forever does. Forever's structure is superior; content just needs to match it.
3. **Blog/guide content is Save On's unique edge.** We should consider (future phase, out of scope here) a small set of guide pages targeting long-tail queries.
4. **Forever's guarantees (125% cancellation, on-time setup, early-pay discount) + online 24/7 booking are unmatched** USPs — these should be surfaced prominently on every city page.
5. **NAP consistency matters.** Competitors all list phone + address plainly above the fold. Forever currently omits address/phone from city pages; schema LocalBusiness + visible NAP block is a must.
6. **Content length varies wildly** — some pages are ~500 words, others push 1,500+ with blog content. Forever should aim for 1,200-1,500 visible words on each city page with unique intros + FAQs.

## Strategic Summary — What to bake into every Forever city page

Consolidated takeaways across Steps 1.1–1.4, prioritized:

1. **JSON-LD Schema (biggest single gap across all competitors):**
   - `LocalBusiness` (NAP + geo + hours + priceRange "$$") — nobody has this properly
   - `Service` with `areaServed` = city name — nobody has this
   - `AggregateRating` (5.0, 200+ reviews) — nobody has this → instant rich-snippet win
   - `FAQPage` with 4-6 Q&As — only Pedersen's has FAQ content, on a separate page
   - `BreadcrumbList` — match Forever's visible breadcrumb

2. **Hyper-local content** (beats single-page competitors like Surdel, Simplicity, Rowe):
   - 3-5 named neighborhoods per city
   - 2-3 named venues/parks/landmarks per city
   - City-specific drive-time reassurance ("just over the Golden Ears Bridge")
   - Explicit "We deliver to [neighborhoods]" block

3. **Forever's unique moat — surface prominently on every page:**
   - 24/7 online booking (vs. competitors' quote-only models)
   - 200+ five-star Google reviews (vs. competitors without visible social proof)
   - Guarantees: 125% cancellation protection, 10% early-pay discount, on-time setup (no competitor offers these)
   - Open 7 days a week (Element closed Sundays; most competitors 9-5 M-F)
   - Delivery + setup included — "no surprise fees" (contra Celebration's $70 delivery fee)

4. **FAQ section per page (visible + schema):**
   - 4-6 Q&As
   - Mix universal ("Do you deliver to [City]?", "What's included in the price?", "How far in advance should I book?") with city-specific ("What venues have you served in [City]?", "Can you set up on grass/asphalt in [City]?").

5. **Content length:**
   - Target 1,200-1,500 visible words per page
   - 70% unique per city (reverse current 70% boilerplate ratio)

6. **Tertiary (nice-to-have):**
   - Visible NAP block per page
   - `priceRange` = "$$" in schema
   - Starting-at pricing hint ("tents from $450, chairs from $X") — if user will share pricing
   - OG + Twitter tags (social share hygiene)

7. **Known threats ranked:**
   1. Element Event Solutions — closest architectural match
   2. A1 Party Rentals — deep inventory, geo pages (ugly URLs)
   3. Save On Party Rentals — content marketing / blog strategy
   4. Rowe Events — price transparency
   5. Surdel — incumbent in Surrey with 30 years of trust
